My name is Dylan Rosser. I'm an electrical engineer with a passion for electronics, signals, and systems. I received the B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of New Haven in 2017, and the M.S. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University in 2020. After my undergraduate degree, I worked as an electrical systems designer for large electrical power and control systems in New York City. I worked as part of an elite team of engineers conducting short circuit, coordination, and arc flash studies for mission critical clients, including banking data centers in the financial capital of the world.

While pursuing my masters degree, my renewed focus on analog circuits and data converters led to a concentration on mixed-signal integrated circuit design. As part of the Energy-Efficient Circuits and Systems Group I worked on computational data converters and microelectronic circuits such as this Analog Neural Network. My research interests include low power data converters, RF and audio circuits, and brain-inspired machine learning circuits.
